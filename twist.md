# Twist (M) in TIMDR

## 1. Definition
Twist (M) is the orientation of change.  
It is the angular structure of the gradient field and the fundamental generator of information.

**Twist = orientation of process = the minimal unit of information.**

## 2. Physical meaning
Twist does not require matter, space, or time.  
It can exist in:
- fields,
- waves,
- geometry,
- topology,
- pure change.

Twist is the only structure that can exist everywhere and preserve information.

## 3. Twist → Relation → Information
The core chain:

1. **Twist (M)** — orientation of change  
2. **Relation (I(t))** — interaction of twists (Laplacian of twist)  
3. **Information (R/E)** — stabilized twist patterns (resonance, grid)

This is the physical backbone of TIMDR.

## 4. Möbius and double Möbius
A single Möbius describes the one‑sidedness of twist.  
A double Möbius (M²) describes twist acting on itself, producing:
- interference,
- diffraction,
- stable relational nodes,
- emergent structure.

## 5. Mapping to the TIMDR pipeline
- **T** – momentum  
- **I** – process  
- **M** – twist  
- **I(t)** – relation + time  
- **R** – stabilization  
- **E** – emergence (closure of M²)
