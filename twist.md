# Twist (M) in TIMDR

## 1. Definition
Twist (M) is the orientation of change.  
It is the angular structure of the gradient field and the minimal unit of
information.  
Twist = orientation of process = the elementary generator of structure.


---

## 2. Physical meaning
Twist does not require matter, space, or time.  
It can exist in fields, waves, geometry, topology, or pure change.  
Twist is the only structure that can exist everywhere and preserve information.


---

## 3. Twist → Relation → Information
The fundamental chain:

- **Twist (M)** — orientation of change  
- **Relation I(t)** — interaction of twists (Laplacian of twist)  
- **Information (R/E)** — stabilized twist patterns (resonance, grid)


This is the physical backbone of TIMDR.

---

## 4. Möbius and double Möbius
A single Möbius describes the one‑sidedness of twist.  
A double Möbius (M²) describes twist acting on itself, producing:
interference, diffraction, stable relational nodes, and emergent structure.


---

## 5. τ — continuous twist field
τ is the continuous twist density derived from the Laplacian of the surface:



\[
\tau = \nabla^2 S
\]



Meaning:
- τ measures **how much** twist is present,
- τ is **continuous**,
- τ governs the geometry of Möbius, mobiosotourys, and tetroida.

τ is the *field* of twist.

---

## 6. J — discrete twist operator
J is the operator of twist arising from positional relation:



\[
J = \frac{d\tau}{ds}
\]



Meaning:
- J measures **where** twist changes,
- J is **discrete / point‑like**,
- J marks the transition point of orientation.

J is the *operator* of twist.

---

## 7. Mobiosotourys — torus with Möbius twist
Mobiosotourys is the generalized surface:



\[
\begin{aligned}
x &= (R + r\cos(v + \tfrac{1}{2}u))\cos u, \\
y &= (R + r\cos(v + \tfrac{1}{2}u))\sin u, \\
z &= r\sin(v + \tfrac{1}{2}u)
\end{aligned}
\]



It interpolates between:
- torus (no twist),
- Möbius (one‑sidedness),
- tetroida (singular twist).

---

## 8. Tetroida — singularity of τ
Tetroida is the minimal, stable singularity of twist:

- one‑sided,
- closed by boundary constraints,
- three convex faces,
- maximal τ,
- point of ΔS transition.

Tetroida is the **endpoint** of twist concentration.
